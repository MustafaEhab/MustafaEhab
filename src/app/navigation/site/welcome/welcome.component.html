Marhaba !

This word is used in the Middle East and North Africa (MENA) to welcome guests and friends. It conveys a sincere welcome and warm hospitality. I couldn’t find a better word to use to welcome you all to Egypt for the 4th IAAH MENA region adolescents’ health conference to be held in-person December 7th-9th, 2021. The MENA region has recently witnessed accelerating and evolving interest and action aimed at promoting the health, development and welfare of the 106 million adolescents we are gifted with in the region. During 2014, the region celebrated the establishment of the Arab Coalition for Adolescent Health & Medicine (ACAHM); currently having representatives from 20 (out of 22) Arab countries. Then the IAAH MENA region was established in 2016 and publicized during the first adolescent health conference held in Cairo in May 2017. The conference was a major scientific event that brought 220 high caliber professionals from 26 countries and 12 International organizations under one roof. The second conference was held in Muscat (2018) and the third in Amman (2018), just before Mr. Covid knocked on our doors. This unwelcome guest prevented the region from holding the conference in 2020. However, the Arab Coalition has decided to hold the fourth conference in the most refreshing, relaxing and enjoyable Red Sea city of Hurghada. This city has been the least affected city in the country by the pandemic due to the adoption of consistent strict preventive measures. A dedicated “safety” committee will be advising and working alongside the organizing committee to ensure a safe and healthy environment for all conference participants. Hurghada promises to provide you with plenty of sunshine, sea and white sandy beaches, the three S’s that tourists look for. However, we also promise you two additional S’s; a rewarding and memorable scientific and social experience. We shall strive to make it an opulent scientific event where you will be meeting old friends and making many new ones as well. “Adolescents’ care; leaving no one behind” is the slogan for this three-day conference. These aren’t just words. This is a reality. Responding to the needs and rights of ALL young people in a holistic manner is a basic human right and will no doubt promote their health, development and well-being, as well as protect them from dangers and diseases. Young adults are the future as much as they are the present. We are looking forward to hosting you during this important and informative meeting so that I welcome everyone with a warm and enthusiastic “Marhaba!”.

Mamdouh Wahba - Vice-President, IAAH MENA region - President, ACAHM
